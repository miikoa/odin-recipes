Cornbread is a must-have to accompany a nice hearty pot of chili during the winter months. This particular recipe is a spin-off from the basic Southern-style, skillet baked, cornbreads that you will find. The only difference is that I take it up a notch with spicy jalapenos and fresh kernels of corn. This cornbread remains cake-like with a minimum level of crumbs.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 1/2 cups of corn meal
  • 1/4 cup of white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup of chopped jarred jalapenos
  • 2 boiled or grilled ears of corn

Preparation

  1. Turn on oven to 400 degrees Farenheit.
  2. Measure and mix all dried ingredients in a large bowl.
  3. Measure and mix all wet ingredients in a medium bowl.
  4. Create a medium sized hole in the bowl of dried ingredients and add combined wet ingredients.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of butter to a cast-iron skillet and place in hot oven for 5 minutes.
  6. Pour cornbread batter onto hot skillet and let bake for 25 minutes or until brown.
