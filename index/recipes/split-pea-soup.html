I grew up on Soup Starter and I thought that this was how everyone made soup. It was not until moving to France years later and taking a year long cooking class, 'cuisine traditionelle française', that I realized that this was not the case after all. The recipe that I am presenting here is the one that I learned during that class. Bon appetit!

Ingredients

  • 1 large white onion
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1/2 of celeriac, including the leaves
  • 3 medium-sized chopped carrots
  • 2 medium-sized chopped potatos
  • 2 cups of raw bacon
  • 1 cup of fresh herbs of choice (flat parsely, thyme)
  • a dried branch with laurel leaves
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 cups of dried split peas
  • salt and cracked pepper to taste

Rinse and Chop

  1. finely chop onions, garlic and parsely
  2. rinse, scrub and dice celeric, carrots and potatos
  3. roughly chop bacon into match sticks
  4. rinse thyme and laurel branch
  5. rinse split peas carefully several times watching for tiny pebbles

Sauté

  1. sauté onions and garlic in a Dutch oven on medium heat until they become translucent
  2. add celeric and saute for 3 minutes
  3. add bacon and sauté for 2 minutes
  4. add parsely and saute for 1 minute
  5. add and saute carrots and potatoes for 2 minutes
  6. mix in split peas
  7. add chicken broth making sure that the liquid generously covers all ingredients. If not add more broth.
  8. mix all ingredients
  9. add thyme and laurel branches
  10. allow it to reach a heavy bubble and after cover and let simmer for 90 minutes

Emolsify until smooth and conistent. Top with cream and fresh herbs. This recipes serves four.
