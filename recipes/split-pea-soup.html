pea soup or potage de Saint Germain for a 4 people

Before providing my soup recipe, I must confess that I grewup on soup starter. As a kid, I thought that this was how everyone made soup. After moving to France, I took a year-long cuisine traditionelle française I realized that this was not the case after all. The recipe that I am presenting here is the one that I learned during that class.

Ingredients

  • 1 large white onion
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1/2 of celeriac, including the leaves
  • 3 medium-sized chopped carrots
  • 2 medium-sized chopped potatos
  • 2 cups of raw bacon
  • 1 cup of fresh herbs of choice (flat parsely, thyme)
  • a dried branch with laurel leaves
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 cups of dried split peas
  • salt and cracked pepper to taste

Step 1

Rinse and chop ingredients

  • finely chop onions, garlic and parsely
  • rinse, scrub and dice celeric, carrots and potatos
  • roughly chop bacon into match sticks
  • rinse thyme and laurel branch
  • rinse split peas carefully several times watching for tiny pebbles

  • Step 2

    sauté onions and garlic in a Dutch oven on medium heat until they become translucent

  • Step 3

    add celeric and saute for 3 minutes

  • Step 4

    add bacon and sauté for 2 minutes

  • Step 5

    add parsely and saute for 1 minute

  • Step 6

    add and saute carrots and potatoes for 2 minutes

  • Step 7

    add and mix in split peas

  • Step 8

    add chicken broth making sure that the liquid generously covers all ingredients. If not add more broth.

  • Step 9

    mix all ingredients

  • Step 10

    add thyme and laurel branches

  • Step 11

    allow it to reach a heavy bubble and after cover and let simmer for 90 minutes
