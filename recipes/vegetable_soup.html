pea soup or potage de Saint Germain for a 4 people

Before providing my soup recipe, I must confess that I grewup on soup starter. As a kid, I thought that this was how everyone made soup. After moving to France, I took a year-long cuisine traditionelle fran√ßaise I realized that this was not the case after all. The recipe that I am presenting here is the one that I learned during that class.

Ingredients

  • 1 large white onion
  • 3 cloves of fresh garlic
  • 1/2 of celeriac, including the leaves
  • 2 cups of raw bacon
  • 3 medium-sized chopped carrots
  • 1 cup of fresh herbs of choice (flat parsely, thyme)
  • a dried branch with laurel leaves
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 cups of dried split peas
  • salt and freshly cracked pepper to taste

Preparation

  1. finely chop onion and garlic
  2. wash, scrub, and cube celeric but save leaves
  3. roughly chop bacon into match sticks
  4. wash, scrub and cube carrots
  5. rinse and finely chop fresh herbs stalks included
  6. rinse split peas several times being sure to check for pebbles
